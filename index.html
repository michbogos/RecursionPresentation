Recursion

A novel by Blake Crouch

Presentation by Michael Bogosta

Disclaimer!

It's physically impossible to condense a whole book into a 5-7 minute presentation.

2018

False memory syndrome

The book starts with Barry Sutton. He's a police officer currently on call.

Ann Voss has been suffering from False Memory Syndrome (FMS). Plagued with false memories of a different life, she tries to take her own life.

Barry wants to prevent this.

He fails

Further investigation

Barry visits Joe Behrmann, Anns Husband, who knows more about FMS. He points him to a secret location.

He visits it in hope of finding answers, but finds something even more tempting...

A chance to set things right

Time travel mechanics

  1. Record a memory
  2. Undergo a very stressful experience
  3. The released neurochemicals make your memory feel like reality
  4. You are transported to a new timeline in the past

    5. ...but you die in this timeline

2018

The timelines merge

When timelines merge people get false memories from the other timeline.

Barry's daughter gets false memories of dying.

Barry goes to the secret location once again.

He meets Helena

2007

Helena

Helena is actually from another timeline.

While trying to find a cure for Alzheimers, she invents a way to record memories.

These can be used to time travel.

Marcus Slade

Killed Helena in his timeline to use the time travel chair.

Wants to rebuild the time travel chair with Helenas help.

Questionable morals

Helena escapes from Slade and wants to prevent the invention of the chair

She refuses to work with Slade

However, when the timelines merge, he remembers how to build the time travel chair.

2018

At this point FMS becomes more common.

The location is raided by a SWAT team.

Slade gets killed.

DARPA starts using the time travel device.

Why does a government need time travel?

To rewrite history.

Other governments and organizations replicate the chair.

Now everybody has the power to change history.

Any usage of the chair means mutually assured destruction (Nuclear war).
